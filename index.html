Hezkiel Arya Nanda

Alumni of Calvin College, USA
SPH Sentul City and Pelita Harapan House (PHH) taught me many life lessons. When I lived at PHH, my parents were not there. With freedom, I realized it required me to choose to become responsible and independent.
I also developed good relationships with my teachers, supervisors, and staff members at school. The environment at SPH Sentul City and PHH was loving, caring, and disciplined, rooted in the right foundation. During my first year of college, I found the transition easy and manageable, due to my previous school and dorm life experience.

Nathaniel Gray Sutanto

Assistant Professor of Systematic Theology at Reformed Theological Seminary
A lot of schools are motivated by achieving high academic results. To them, numbers matter more than people. At SPH, people matter more than the numbers.
My time at SPH truly impacted me, and one of the highlights was the caring teachers and community. When I was at SPH, I was a delinquent student, to say the least. I never really listened to my teachers and often fell asleep in class. I now realize that even though I did not listen, a lot of teachers paid attention to me and cared for me. SPH is not a place that expels or rejects you. Instead, it is a community that pursues you because they truly care. They invite you back in to restore you. For example, Mr. Brian Cox, a former Head of School, was my ethics teacher. I once asked a rebellious question in his class. He didnâ€™t shun me but instead invited me for coffee in his office to talk.
